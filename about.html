Overview

Bennett Foddy is a video game developer and an educator. He was born in Australia in 1978 and is currently based in New York City. Foddy is best known for his experimental and challenging games that often have a strong emphasis on physics and skill-based gameplay.

Foddy's games are often inspired by classic arcade style games or sports games, but with a unique twist. For example, "QWOP" is a game in which you control a sprinter's individual leg muscles to move forward, while "Getting Over It" is a game that requires the you to climb a mountain full of trash and obstacles with just a sledgehammer.

In addition to his game development work, Foddy is also an active commentator on the video game industry and on video game culture. He has written articles for numerous places, including The New Yorker and The Guardian, and has appeared on many podcasts and panels to discuss game development and game design.

Early Life and Education

Bennett Foddy was born in Adelaide, Australia, in 1978, and he spent his childhood in Melbourne. Foddy received a Bachelor of Arts degree with honors in philosophy from the University of Melbourne. He went on to earn a PhD in philosophy from Princeton University and a Master of Arts in Philosophy from Oxford.

Foddy became interested in video games, and started making them as a hobby while he was at Oxford. After finishing his PhD, he moved to the United States to work as a researcher for the Department of Philosophy at the University of California, Los Angeles. He continued to make games in his spare time while working here.

Due to their deliberately difficult and absurd gameplay, Foddy's early games, such as "QWOP" and "GIRP," gained a cult following. The innovative design and distinctive gameplay mechanics of Foddy's later games, such as "Getting Over It with Bennett Foddy," have received widespread praise from critics. Alongside his work in the game development field, Foddy also went on to publish research on the philosophy of games and on the ethics of game design.

Career

Bennett Foddy works as a video game designer at New York University. He is most popular for his creative and difficult games, which have acquired a clique following for their innovative gameplay mechanics and overall absurdity.

Foddy started out as a philosopher. He got his PhD from Princeton University and did postdoctoral research at the University of California, Los Angeles. After developing an interest in video games, he began creating games as a hobby during this time. His early games, like "QWOP" and "GIRP," were developed at this time. They became popular because of their unconventional gameplay and deliberately difficult mechanics.

"Getting Over It," one of Foddy's later games, has received critical acclaim for its innovative design and novel gameplay mechanics. It has been praised for its extreme difficulty and minimalist design, which requires players to climb a mountain with only a sledgehammer.

Alongside his work as a game developer, and dipping into his rich background in philosophy, Foddy has also contributed research on the philosophy of games and on the ethics of game design. Since 2012, he has taught game design at the Tisch School of the Arts at New York University. In 2019, he was given a Guggenheim Fellowship for his work in game design.
